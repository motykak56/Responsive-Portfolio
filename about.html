Education
UK, WKU, Vanderbilt, SMU, Villanova

I have attended some fine institutions of learning. And I've learned a great deal in the process. Having two undergraduate degrees, one in Engineering and the other in Music, I've got a well-rounded education in both science and the arts.

Work Experience
I've worked at a host of different companies....small, medium and large.

I have however been fired from the past 3 jobs I've held. I always end up upsetting the apple cart by questioning the status quo. I have come to the conclusion that it's time to quit working for other people and start working for myself. I'm way too independent of a thinker to work for others and I'm getting more so as I get older. I've waited a very long time for this and I'm excited about it. My big-picture ideas will no longer be thwarted by others. I will be solely responsible for them.

Career Trajectory
Musician to Engineer to Web Developer

I went into music as a profession quite by accident. My parents told me I had to get a scholarship in order to go away to college and a music scholarship was easy to come by. I was talented as a musician and I just got sucked in. I went into Engineering because I wanted to be respected on the job and I figured I could get the degree if I worked hard enough. I am now going into Web Development because I want to do the work. I like coding. I can do it for hours on end and never tire of it. I believe I'm properly motivated this time and with enough hard work I will become talented at this as well. It's an outlet for my energy and it opens the door allowing me to do interesting things in my solitude which I value so much.

Family

I don't have much family I'm close to especially now that my mother is gone. I have a beloved granddaughter and grandson. They're 3 and 2 years old and cute as buttons. I have one child, a son who has been cruel to me since he was 11 years old and never got over it. He is what stands between me and knowing my grandchildren. I have an uncle in Miami who is 93 years old and precious to me. I have a sister and three nieces...I love my nieces as if they were my own daughters but they all have their own lives now. I have one ex husband who is a god-send in time of need. He's one of the most helpful people in my life...no regrets about the divorce but he's an excellent human being all around yet he has a live-in girlfriend so I'm careful how often I contact him and for what reason. I want to respect their relationship. Finally, I would be wrong to omit my 2 dogs: Peppa and Parker who love me unconditionally and I them. They are my immediate family and provide me a fullness I wouldn't have without them. So that's my family. A bit dysfunctional but probably most American families are.
